# Writing Rules and Harvard Resume Standards

## Harvard Resume Format Requirements

### Overall Structure
- **Length**: 1 page for most roles, 2 pages maximum for senior positions (10+ years)
- **Margins**: 0.5-1 inch on all sides
- **Font**: Professional fonts (Times New Roman, Arial, Calibri) 10-12pt
- **Spacing**: Single-spaced within sections, double-spaced between sections
- **Alignment**: Left-aligned text, consistent formatting throughout

### Section Order (Harvard Standard)
1. **Header**: Name, contact information, LinkedIn/portfolio links
2. **Education**: Degree, institution, graduation date, relevant coursework/honors
3. **Experience**: Professional roles in reverse chronological order
4. **Skills**: Technical and relevant professional skills
5. **Projects**: Relevant projects (if space permits)
6. **Additional**: Certifications, awards, languages (if relevant)

### Header Format
```
[Full Name]
[Phone] | [Email] | [City, State] | [LinkedIn URL] | [Portfolio/GitHub URL]
```

## Bullet Point Construction (Harvard Style)

### Structure Requirements
- Start with strong action verb in past tense (completed roles) or present tense (current role)
- Follow CAR format: Context + Action + Result
- Quantify impact with specific metrics whenever possible
- Keep bullets to one line maximum (Harvard standard)
- Use parallel structure within each role

### Action Verb Categories (Harvard Approved)
**Leadership**: Led, Managed, Directed, Coordinated, Supervised, Mentored, Spearheaded
**Creation**: Built, Developed, Designed, Created, Implemented, Established, Launched
**Improvement**: Optimized, Enhanced, Streamlined, Reduced, Increased, Improved, Accelerated
**Analysis**: Analyzed, Evaluated, Assessed, Investigated, Researched, Identified, Diagnosed
**Collaboration**: Collaborated, Partnered, Facilitated, Coordinated, Negotiated

### Harvard Quantification Standards
Every bullet should include measurable impact:
- **Performance**: "Reduced API response time 40% from 200ms to 120ms, improving user satisfaction"
- **Scale**: "Managed infrastructure serving 10M+ daily users across 15 global regions"
- **Financial**: "Delivered $2M annual cost savings through infrastructure automation initiatives"
- **Team**: "Led cross-functional team of 8 engineers across 3 time zones for 18-month project"
- **Time**: "Completed critical migration 3 weeks ahead of schedule, enabling Q4 product launch"
- **Quality**: "Achieved 99.9% uptime SLA, exceeding industry standard by 0.4%"

## Harvard Education Section Standards

### Format Requirements
- **Order**: Institution, Degree, Graduation Date, Location
- **GPA**: Include only if 3.5+ (Harvard recommendation)
- **Coursework**: List 3-4 most relevant courses for target role
- **Honors**: Dean's List, Magna Cum Laude, academic awards

### Example Format
```
Harvard University, Cambridge, MA
Bachelor of Arts in Computer Science, May 2020
Relevant Coursework: Data Structures, Algorithms, Software Engineering, Database Systems
Honors: Magna Cum Laude, Dean's List (4 semesters)
```

## Harvard Experience Section Standards

### Role Format
```
[Job Title] | [Company Name] | [Location] | [Start Date - End Date]
• [Action verb] + [context] + [quantified result]
• [Action verb] + [context] + [quantified result]
• [Action verb] + [context] + [quantified result]
```

### Harvard Bullet Guidelines
- **Maximum 4 bullets per role** (Harvard standard for readability)
- **One line per bullet** (essential for Harvard format)
- **Most impactful first** (lead with strongest achievements)
- **Parallel structure** (consistent verb tenses and formats)
- **No personal pronouns** (avoid I, me, my, we, us)

## ATS-Friendly Formatting (Harvard Compatible)

### Formatting Rules
- Use standard fonts: Times New Roman, Arial, or Calibri
- Consistent bullet points (• or -) throughout document
- No graphics, images, or complex formatting
- Standard section headers without decorative elements
- Simple, clean layout that scans well

### Keyword Optimization
- Include industry-standard terminology naturally in context
- Use both spelled-out and abbreviated forms when appropriate
- Match target role requirements with relevant experience
- Integrate technical skills within experience descriptions

## Harvard Skills Section Standards

### Technical Skills Format
- **Group by category**: Programming Languages, Frameworks, Tools, Platforms
- **Proficiency levels**: Expert, Advanced, Intermediate (optional)
- **Relevance order**: Most relevant to target role first
- **Concise presentation**: Avoid lengthy descriptions

### Example Skills Section
```
Technical Skills: Python, Java, JavaScript, React, Node.js, AWS, Docker, Kubernetes
Tools & Platforms: Git, Jenkins, Terraform, PostgreSQL, MongoDB, Linux, Agile/Scrum
```

## Content Quality Standards (Harvard Requirements)

### Acceptable Metrics (Harvard Style)
- **Specific numbers with context**: "Reduced deployment time from 2 hours to 15 minutes"
- **Percentage improvements**: "Increased system efficiency by 35% through code optimization"
- **Scale indicators**: "Processed 1M+ daily transactions with 99.9% accuracy"
- **Financial impact**: "Generated $500K additional revenue through feature optimization"
- **Time-based achievements**: "Delivered critical feature 2 weeks ahead of deadline"

### Unacceptable Content (Harvard Standards)
- **Vague quantities**: "significantly improved", "greatly reduced", "many"
- **Unverifiable claims**: "best-in-class performance", "world-class"
- **Relative comparisons without context**: "50% better than before"
- **Responsibilities without impact**: "responsible for managing team"
- **Technical jargon without business context**: "optimized algorithms"

### Harvard Tone and Voice Guidelines
- **Active voice consistently**: "Led team of 5" not "Team of 5 was led by me"
- **Confident but factual tone**: Avoid superlatives and exaggeration
- **Business impact focus**: Connect technical work to business outcomes
- **Professional language**: Industry-appropriate terminology without slang
- **Concise communication**: Every word should add value

## TODO Marker Protocol (Harvard Compliance)

### When to Use TODO Markers
- **Missing specific metrics**: "Reduced processing time by TODO% through optimization"
- **Uncertain dates**: "TODO: verify exact start date"
- **Incomplete quantification**: "Led team of TODO engineers on critical project"
- **Pending verification**: "Achieved TODO% uptime improvement"

### Harvard Accuracy Requirements
- **All dates must be accurate**: Month/year format for consistency
- **Company names exact**: Match official legal names and capitalization
- **Job titles precise**: Use official titles from employment records
- **Achievements verifiable**: All claims must be supportable with evidence
- **No fabrication policy**: Missing information marked with TODO, never invented

## Harvard Resume Length Guidelines

### Standard Length Requirements
- **0-5 years experience**: Strictly 1 page
- **5-10 years experience**: 1 page preferred, 2 pages maximum
- **10+ years experience**: 2 pages maximum
- **Executive level**: 2 pages standard

### Content Prioritization for Length
- **Most recent 5-7 years**: Full detail with 3-4 bullets per role
- **Earlier experience**: Condensed to 1-2 bullets focusing on progression
- **Education**: Brief for experienced professionals, detailed for recent graduates
- **Skills**: Concise list of most relevant technical competencies
